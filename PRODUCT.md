# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** Students in Class 6–12 (IGCSE, IB, CBSE, JEE, NEET, Olympiad) in Jaipur and nearby cities, discovering coaching options and evaluating Hodu Academy against competitors.

**Secondary:** Parents who are the decision-makers — they compare fees, faculty credibility, results, and batch quality before enrolling their child.

**Context:** Both arrive via search or word-of-mouth, often on mobile, comparing 2–3 institutes simultaneously. They need quick proof of credibility (results, faculty, testimonials) and a frictionless way to enquire or enroll.

## Product Purpose

Hodu Academy's public website exists to convert visitors into enrolled students. It does this by establishing academic credibility, showcasing toppers and results, presenting courses clearly with pricing and features, and making enquiry/enrollment effortless. The admin CMS allows staff to manage course listings, leads, notices, faculty, and testimonials without touching code.

## Brand Personality

Energetic · Ambitious · Modern

The voice is confident and motivating — like a coach who believes in every student's potential. Not corporate, not cold. Copy should feel like a mentor speaking, not a brochure.

## Anti-references

- **Old-school institute sites** — plain HTML tables, no visual hierarchy, scanned PDFs as "downloads", zero whitespace. Hodu must feel categorically different: premium, intentional, designed.
- Generic EdTech discount banners (flashing "90% OFF", cluttered hero sections with 12 competing CTAs).

## Design Principles

1. **Results first** — Social proof (toppers, scores, testimonials) should be prominent and visible without scrolling deep. Don't bury the evidence.
2. **Clarity over completeness** — Every page should answer one key question. Courses page: "what's available and what does it cost?" Contact: "how do I reach someone?" Don't dump everything on one page.
3. **Speed of trust** — Faculty credentials, batch sizes, pass rates — surface the numbers that build trust fast. A parent decides in 90 seconds.
4. **Mobile-first urgency** — Most visitors are on phones. CTAs should be reachable with one thumb, forms should be short, and key info shouldn't require horizontal scroll.
5. **Premium without pretension** — Use dark navy + deep maroon as anchors. Restraint over decoration. One strong accent, not five competing colors.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target: WCAG AA
- Ensure all form inputs have visible labels and error states
- Color contrast ratio ≥ 4.5:1 for body text, ≥ 3:1 for large text
- All interactive elements keyboard-navigable
- Avoid relying on color alone to convey information (use icons + labels)
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for marquee and pulse animations
